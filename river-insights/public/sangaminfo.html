Krishna-Koyna

The Krishna-Koyna Sangam is the confluence of the Krishna and Koyna rivers near Karad in Maharashtra, India. This picturesque spot is known for its serene beauty, where the waters of the two rivers merge. The Krishna River, one of India's longest rivers, and the Koyna River, a significant tributary, both originate in the Western Ghats.

Krishna-Panchganga

The Krishna-Panchganga Sangam is the confluence of the Krishna and Panchganga rivers located in Kurundwad, near Kolhapur in Maharashtra, India. This confluence is a notable site for its picturesque and serene environment, where the two rivers merge. The Krishna River, one of India's major rivers, joins with the Panchganga River, which is formed by the union of five smaller rivers.

Godavari-Pravara

The Godavari-Pravara Sangam is the confluence of the Godavari and Pravara rivers near Pravara Sangam village in the Ahmednagar district of Maharashtra, India. The Godavari River, often referred to as the "Ganga of the South," is one of India's longest rivers, while the Pravara River is a significant tributary originating from the Western Ghats. This confluence is known for its serene and scenic beauty, attracting visitors and devotees who come for its spiritual significance and natural charm.
